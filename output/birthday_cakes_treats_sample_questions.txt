KIDS 4 AND UNDER (Basic sensory and vocabulary focus)
=============================================================================

      Answer: What is a candy cane?

$400: "This is what you light on your birthday cake"
      Answer: What are candles?

$600: "These tiny colored balls sit on top of cupcakes"
      Answer: What are sprinkles (or nonpareils)?

$800: "This sweet white powder goes on top of cake"
      Answer: What is powdered sugar?

$1000: "You make a wish before blowing these out"
       Answer: What are birthday candles?

KIDS 10 AND UNDER (Recipe, ingredients, and basic chemistry focus)
=============================================================================

$200: "This ancient Roman honey cake evolved into modern birthday cakes"
      Answer: What is panis mellitus?

$400: "This process combines butter and sugar to create fluffy frosting"
      Answer: What is creaming?

$600: "This chemical reaction creates brownness in cakes"
      Answer: What is the Maillard reaction?

$800: "This ratio of flour to butter to sugar makes perfect shortbread"
      Answer: What is 3:2:1?

$1000: "This Mexican wedding cookie is rolled in powdered sugar"
       Answer: What are polvorones (or Mexican wedding cookies)?

=============================================================================

      Answer: What is 50-70%?

$400: "This molecular structure makes butter superior to margarine"
      Answer: What is triglyceride formation?

$600: "This French term means 'putting in place' all ingredients"
      Answer: What is mise en place?

$800: "This starch retrogradation causes cakes to go stale over time"
      Answer: What is amylopectin recrystallization?

$1000: "This water activity level preserves cake freshness"
       Answer: What is 0.3-0.4?

ADULTS (Food history, culture, and scholarship)
=============================================================================

$200: "This medieval guild regulated spice cake and gingerbread production in Europe"
      Answer: What is the Lebkuchner guild?

$400: "This thermodynamic principle explains oven spring during baking"
      Answer: What is ideal gas law expansion?

$600: "This concept by Pierre Bourdieu explains birthday cakes as cultural capital"
      Answer: What is habitus?

$800: "This anthropologist studied gift-giving rituals including birthday cakes"
      Answer: Who is Mary Douglas?

$1000: "This philosopher's aesthetics framework applies to cake decoration appreciation"
       Answer: Who is Immanuel Kant?

=============================================================================
NO HUMANITY (Mathematical and theoretical frameworks)
=============================================================================

$200: "This partial differential equation models heat diffusion in birthday cakes"
      Answer: What is the Fourier heat equation?

$400: "This tensor field describes stress distribution in cake batter"
      Answer: What is the Cauchy stress tensor?

$600: "This quantum mechanical model explains caramelization reactions"
      Answer: What is density functional theory (DFT)?

$800: "This computational fluid dynamics models convection in baking ovens"
      Answer: What is Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS)?

$1000: "This optimal transport theory moves ingredients most efficiently"
       Answer: What is Wasserstein metric?
