## Key Principles:

1. **Cultural Touchstones (2000-2025)**
   - Social media platforms (Instagram, TikTok, OnlyFans, Twitter/X)
   - Streaming (Netflix, Hulu, Disney+)
   - Apps (Uber, DoorDash, Venmo, Tinder, Bumble)
   - Memes and viral trends
   - Political moments (Trump, COVID, elections)
   - Celebrity culture (Kardashians, influencers)
   - Tech companies (Amazon, Apple, Google)

2. **Make It Make Sense**
   - The answer should be something people actually know
   - The connection to Christmas should be clear
   - The humor should land (not just be random crude words)

3. **CAH Format**
   - Short, punchy answers
   - Fill-in-the-blank style
   - Unexpected but logical connections
   - Dark humor that's clever, not just gross

4. **Avoid:**
   - Random academic/technical terms
   - Obscure references nobody gets
   - Word salad that doesn't mean anything
   - Being crude just for crude's sake without humor

## The Golden Rule:
**If your boomer parents wouldn't get the reference, it's too obscure.**
**If the punchline is just "penis" or "cocaine" without context, it's not funny.**
**If it sounds like a physics textbook, you're doing it wrong.**

## CRITICAL RULE #1: MAINSTREAM REFERENCES ONLY

**If your parents or someone who doesn't use Reddit wouldn't know what it is, DON'T USE IT.**

### The "Would My Mom Know This?" Test
Before using ANY reference, ask:
- ✅ Has this been on mainstream TV/news?
- ✅ Does it have millions of users/followers?
- ✅ Would someone over 50 recognize it?
- ✅ Has SNL made jokes about it?

### ALWAYS USE (Everyone Knows These):
- **Apps**: Amazon, Netflix, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, Uber, DoorDash, Venmo, Spotify
- **Tech**: iPhone, Alexa, Google, Tesla, Zoom
- **People**: Trump, Biden, Taylor Swift, Elon Musk, Kardashians, Mariah Carey
- **Events**: COVID-19, pandemic, lockdown, vaccines, student loans, inflation
- **Brands**: Starbucks, Target, Walmart, McDonald's, Costco
- **Memes**: Karen, OK Boomer, "main character energy" (if explaining it in context)

### NEVER USE (Too Obscure):
- ❌ Deep Reddit/Discord memes
- ❌ Niche podcast references
- ❌ Indie games/anime most people don't know
- ❌ Academic or technical jargon
- ❌ "Extremely online" references

## CRITICAL RULE #2: Clue-Answer Hinting Technique

**The clue must HINT at the answer like a joke setup hints at the punchline.**

### Bad Example (Too Direct):
- "Santa's real workshop location" → "What is a Chinese sweatshop?"
- Problem: Clue doesn't hint, just states

### Good Example (Proper Hinting):
- "Where Santa actually sources his 'Made in the North Pole' toys for $0.03 per unit" → "What is a Chinese sweatshop?"
- Why it works: Hints through cheap manufacturing + label deception

### Hinting Techniques:
1. **Context Setup**: Start with scenario that points toward answer
2. **Specific Details**: Include recognizable details (warehouse = Amazon, data harvesting = Facebook)
3. **Logical Bridge**: Create clear connection, not random association
4. **Foreshadowing**: Plant clues that make answer feel inevitable

### More Examples:

**Setup → Payoff Structure:**
- "The gig economy platform Santa secretly drives for at 2am on December 26th to pay off Christmas debt" → "What is DoorDash?"
  - Hints: gig economy + 2am + delivery = DoorDash

- "This giant tech company might be delivering your Christmas presents from the warehouse down the street instead of the North Pole" → "What is Amazon?"
  - Hints: warehouse + delivery + replacing traditional = Amazon

- "The app Santa uses to track naughty kids by harvesting their parents' data" → "What is Facebook?"
  - Hints: tracking + data harvesting = Facebook

**NOT like this:**
- "Santa's delivery system" → "What is Amazon?" (too vague, doesn't hint)
- "Social media" → "What is Facebook?" (not a hint, just category)
